# The World of Solara

## Overview

Solara is a warm, fantastical world of rolling meadows, ancient forests, volcanic coasts, and crystalline tundra. It is not a dark world by nature — quite the opposite. Solara was made to be bright. But something is changing, and the light is fading.

The world is mid-sized: seven distinct regions encircle a central sacred plateau called the **Beacon Heartland**, where the Lightweavers once gathered. Each region is separated by natural geography — mountain passes, river crossings, sea channels — but they have always been connected by the warmth of the Beacon Towers, visible on clear nights from almost anywhere in Solara.

---

## The Lightweavers

Long before living memory, Solara was shaped by beings called the **Lightweavers** — not gods, but something close. They were architects of luminance, able to weave sunlight, starlight, and inner fire into permanent structures. Their greatest achievement was the **Seven Beacon Towers**, one placed in each of Solara's regions, each tuned to the specific light of that land: the warm amber of the wheat plains, the cool blue-white of the glacier peaks, the deep gold of the canyon deserts.

The Lightweavers did not rule. They tended. They walked between regions, maintaining the Towers, befriending the creatures called Lumins who gathered around the light. The Lumins, in turn, kept the ecosystems balanced — they were drawn to the Towers' glow and carried that energy into the forests, rivers, and skies.

Then, approximately three hundred years before the game begins, the Lightweavers vanished. No war, no disaster — they simply stopped appearing. The Towers they left behind continued to burn for generations. But without a Lightweaver's hand to tend them, the Beacons slowly began to dim.

---

## The Beacon Towers

Each Beacon Tower is a tall, elegant stone spire built in the architectural style of its region. At the top burns an eternal flame — or it did. The flame is not ordinary fire; it is woven from **Lightweaver essence**, a form of living light that cannot be rekindled by ordinary means.

When a Beacon burns:
- The surrounding region is bathed in warmth. Colors are vivid, saturated, alive.
- Lumins are calm and curious. Many gather near towns, becoming beloved companions to locals.
- The land is fertile. Weather is gentle. Shadow creatures cannot form.

When a Beacon dims or goes dark:
- The region grows visually desaturated — not pitch-black, but dull, grey-tinged, muted.
- Lumins grow skittish and retreat into hiding, or worse, become anxious and aggressive.
- Shadow creatures — beings formed from accumulated darkness — emerge from caves, deep forests, and ancient ruins.
- The local people feel a low, persistent unease. Crops thin. Nights feel longer.

---

## Light and Darkness

Light in Solara is not metaphorical — it has physical, magical properties.

**Living light** (Lightweaver-touched): Found in Beacon flames, in Luma's inner spark, and in the hearts of Lumins. It sustains, heals, and repels shadow creatures. It cannot be snuffed by ordinary means — only starved by the absence of a Lightweaver to maintain it.

**Ambient light**: Sunlight, moonlight, firelight. Normal and plentiful, but it does not hold shadow creatures at bay. A candle provides comfort but not protection.

**Shadow**: Not darkness itself, but a creeping corruption that fills spaces where living light has been absent too long. Shadow given enough time and space takes on form — these are the **Umbral creatures**, twisted reflections of Lumins that never got to see the light. They are not evil so much as incomplete and afraid.

## The Beacon Heartland

At the center of Solara sits a high plateau accessible only after the player has relit several Beacon Towers. This is where the Lightweavers gathered, where their records are kept in stone, and where the truth about their disappearance — and the source of the growing darkness — waits to be discovered.

The Heartland's own Beacon, the **Prime Beacon**, is not merely dark — it has been sealed. Something closed it deliberately, trapping the accumulated light of all the Lightweavers' work. Releasing it is the final act of the game.

## Solara's People

The people of Solara are ordinary humans, descendants of those who lived alongside the Lightweavers. They do not have magic themselves, but many of them have deep bonds with Lumins, and Lightkeepers — a profession, not a magical gift — traditionally maintain the Beacon Towers by cleaning the lenses, trimming the mechanical parts, and recording the Beacons' behavior.

When Beacons began going dark, the Lightkeeper order scrambled. Most Lightkeepers alive today have never seen a Tower they could actually tend — they arrived to find the flame already gone. They've become historians and guards of cold stone rather than tenders of living fire.

Luma's mentor, **Elder Cerin**, is one such Lightkeeper — old enough to remember when Willowfen's Beacon burned bright, young enough to feel the shame of its dimming on his watch.
