# Characters

## Luma (Protagonist)

**Age**: 16
**Role**: Light-Carrier, Lightkeeper apprentice
**Home**: Willowfen

Luma is warm, curious, and a little too quick to act before thinking. She's spent her childhood cataloging Lumins with Elder Cerin and has a naturalist's eye — she notices things others miss, especially about creatures and places. She's not particularly brave at the start; she volunteers for the journey because she can't stand the idea of someone else suffering when she might be able to help.

Her defining quality is *attentiveness*. She listens. She asks questions. She remembers the name of every Lumin she meets. This makes her beloved everywhere she goes — and makes each parting harder.

**Arc**: Luma begins unsure of her own light, treating it as a tool she's been given rather than a part of herself. Over the journey she learns that the light isn't separate from her — it's the expression of who she already is. By the end, she doesn't wield the light; she *is* it.

**Visual Notes**: Small and wiry, short dark hair with a warm amber glow that occasionally flickers at her fingertips when she's emotional. Practical traveling clothes in earthy greens and browns. Carries a worn leather notebook. Her Lumin companion Pip often rides on her shoulder.

## Pip (Companion Lumin)

**Type**: Ember-moth Lumin (Flamewing)
**Bond**: Formed the moment Luma's light first appeared

Pip is Luma's first and closest Lumin companion — an ember-moth no larger than a fist, with wings like stained glass and a warm glow that pulses when excited. Pip communicates in chirps, wing-beats, and an extremely expressive face.

Pip is the game's comic heart. Wildly enthusiastic, occasionally cowardly, deeply loyal. Pip will face down an Umbral Guardian but refuses to enter water under any circumstances. Pip also has an inexplicable obsession with shiny objects and has been responsible for at least three diplomatic incidents.

In gameplay terms, Pip is the player's starting Lumin and grows with Luma throughout the entire game. Pip's evolution line reflects the journey: humble ember-moth → radiant sun-moth → the **Solwing**, a creature of pure living light.

## Elder Cerin (Mentor)

**Age**: Late 60s
**Role**: Lightkeeper of Willowfen, Luma's teacher

Cerin is a gentle, slightly rumpled man who has dedicated his life to a job he was never quite able to do — he arrived in Willowfen to tend the Beacon only to find it already half-dim. He knows more than anyone alive about the Lightweaver texts, but knowledge without light is cold comfort.

He sends Luma into the world with profound guilt and profound pride in equal measure. He stays in Willowfen to keep the community together and provide Luma with research support via letter (delivered by Lumins).

**Role in gameplay**: Tutorial character, quest giver for the first region, and source of lore letters delivered at each inn.

---

## Sable (Traveling Companion)

**Age**: 18
**Role**: Wanderer, former apprentice to the Frostmere Lightkeeper

Luma meets Sable in the Ashfields (Region 2) — an older teenager traveling in the opposite direction, heading home to Frostmere after years away. Sable is dry-witted, self-sufficient, and skeptical of Luma's optimism. They argue. They become friends.

Sable eventually turns around and travels with Luma — not because they believe in the mission at first, but because they're curious, and because they haven't been back to Frostmere in years and want to see it again properly.

**Arc**: Sable grew up in a place with no Beacon, no light, no frame of reference for what Luma is chasing. Watching the first Beacon rekindle changes something in them permanently. By the time they reach Frostmere, Sable is the most fiercely committed member of the group.

**Role in gameplay**: Second party member, joined after the Ashfields quest. Controls in co-op mode. Has their own Lumin bond (a frost-sprite named **Sleet**).

## Solen (The Antagonist — Sympathetic)

**Age**: Unknowable (has existed inside the sealed Beacon for ~300 years)
**Role**: The last Lightweaver; the cause of the Unraveling; the final encounter

Solen is not a villain. He is the most tragic figure in the game — a brilliant Lightweaver who tried to do something heroic (amplify the Prime Beacon to reverse the dimming he'd noticed beginning) and caused the catastrophe he was trying to prevent.

He has spent three centuries inside the sealed Beacon, half-consumed by the shadow he unleashed, slowly losing coherence. The Umbral Guardians that appear across Solara are his fragmented attempts to reach out — distorted projections of his desperation to communicate.

When Luma finally reaches him, Solen is barely recognizable as a person. But he's still in there. And what he needs is not defeat — he needs someone to finally *reach* him.

**Visual Notes**: The Umbral Guardians across the game are thematically linked to Solen — each one subtly resembles a different Lumin type, hinting at his nature. The final encounter reveals a figure of light and shadow in equal measure.

## The Umbral Guardians (Bosses)

One per region. Each is a massive shadow creature formed from the accumulated darkness of a long-dark Beacon zone. They are not evil — they are the darkness made manifest, incomplete and afraid. Defeating them is sad as much as triumphant; once the Beacon is relit, the Umbral creature dissolves peacefully rather than violently.

| Region | Umbral Guardian | Form | Theme |
|---|---|---|---|
| Willowfen | The Murk | Enormous shadowy river serpent | Loss, stagnation |
| Ashfields | The Hollow Shepherd | Giant figure made of dry grain and dust | Isolation, abandonment |
| Tidehollow | The Drowned Bell | Vast shape like a sunken ship, ringing silence | Forgotten things |
| Emberveil | The Smothered Flame | A fire creature whose light has been choked | Suppressed passion |
| Verdant Tangle | The Canopy Void | A living absence that darkens everything it touches | Forgetting |
| Frostmere | The Long Night | Immense, ancient, barely a shape at all | Acceptance of hopelessness |
| Beacon Heartland | Solen (the Unraveled) | Light and shadow intertwined, impossible to read | Resolution |
