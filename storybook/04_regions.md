# The Seven Regions of Solara

Each region entry includes: the biome, its people and culture, the current state of the Beacon, the Beacon Shard's hiding place, the Umbral Guardian, and the transformation that occurs when the Beacon is relit.

---

## Region 1 — Willowfen

**Biome**: River delta, wetlands, reed marshes
**Culture**: Quiet river-folk, fishers, herbalists, Lumin naturalists
**Beacon State at game start**: Flickering — went dark during the opening scene

### Before the Beacon

Willowfen is dim and anxious. The reeds are grey-green. The river runs brown. Lumins have retreated into the deeper marshes. Shadow sprites appear near the mills at night. The villagers lock their doors early and speak in low voices.

### The Beacon Shard

Hidden beneath the roots of the **Willowroot** — an enormous ancient willow tree at the heart of the wetlands that the Lightweavers planted as an anchor. The Murk, a shadow river serpent, has coiled around it. Tutorial region: the dungeon is small and introduces combat and capture mechanics gently.

### The Umbral Guardian — The Murk

A vast, slow-moving shadow serpent made of dark water and silence. It does not attack — it *smothers*. It wraps itself around the Willowroot and pulses with a deep, sad sound. Defeating it reveals the Shard cradled in the roots like an egg.

### After the Beacon

Willowfen becomes the game's most emotionally resonant before/after. The player has seen it in colour during the opening flashback. Rekindling the Beacon recreates that warmth — golden light rippling across the water, fireflies emerging, Lumins splashing in the shallows, Elder Cerin weeping quietly on the tower steps.

## Region 2 — The Ashfields

**Biome**: Rolling farmland, grassland, dusty plains
**Culture**: Farming communities, once prosperous, now militia-organized against Umbral threats
**Beacon State**: Dark for 2 seasons

### Before the Beacon

The Ashfields are a washed-out tan and grey. Crops grow thin. Wind raises dust devils of dead grass. The farming villages have banded together under Captain Harrow's informal militia — they survive, but barely. There is significant inter-village tension about resource sharing.

### The Beacon Shard

Buried under the foundation of the old **Ashfields Waystation** — a Lightkeeper relay point that was abandoned when the Beacon went dark. The building has since become the base of operations for the worst of the Umbral Shepherds. Recovering the Shard requires navigating the waystation dungeon while also resolving the tensions between Harrow's militia faction and a dissenting village that wants to negotiate with the Umbral creatures.

### The Umbral Guardian — The Hollow Shepherd

A towering figure that looks almost like a person made of compacted dry grass, dust, and the shapes of former crops. It wanders the fields at night and drives Lumins away with its approach. It is profoundly lonely — it moves as if looking for something.

### After the Beacon

The Ashfields explode with colour — the grain turns gold, wildflowers emerge seemingly from nowhere, the sky goes from washed-out white to vivid blue. The militia dissolves. The village dispute resolves immediately; they were so tense from the darkness. Lumins pour out of the grasslands. Harrow laughs for the first time in a year.

## Region 3 — Tidehollow

**Biome**: Coastal cliffs, sea caves, tidal beaches, small harbour town
**Culture**: Maritime, explorer-descended, deeply superstitious, oral history keepers
**Beacon State**: Dark for 1 year

### Before the Beacon

Tidehollow is a cold, misty grey. The sea reflects a colourless sky. The harbour town is mostly functional — fishing continues — but the sea caves have become Umbral territory and two fishing crews have gone missing in them. Captain Vesper, a retired explorer with Lightweaver maps, has been trying to keep morale up through storytelling, but even she is running low on hope.

### The Beacon Shard

Hidden in the deepest sea cave, submerged in a tidal pool at the center of the cave system. The caves flood and drain with the tides, requiring timed navigation. The Drowned Bell haunts the caves.

### The Umbral Guardian — The Drowned Bell

An enormous shape that resembles a sunken ship — or perhaps just the absence of a ship. It moves without sound except for a deep, resonant *clang* that seems to come from inside the water itself. It does not fight conventionally; it creates zones of darkness that must be pushed back with Lumin light.

### After the Beacon

Tidehollow's Beacon produces a cool, sea-blue light unique among the Towers. The mist clears. The sea shifts to vivid teal. The sea caves, flooded with light through cracks in the rock ceiling, become spectacular — locals will discover them properly for the first time. Vesper gives Luma her most important Lightweaver map, pointing toward the Beacon Heartland.

## Region 4 — Emberveil

**Biome**: Volcanic highlands, lava fields, hot springs, ash forests
**Culture**: Fire-worshippers who misidentified volcanic energy as sacred — a cult built on misunderstanding what living light actually is
**Beacon State**: Dark for 3 years

### Before the Beacon

Emberveil is already a dramatic landscape — volcanic, red-black rock, permanent haze. In darkness, it becomes oppressive. The cult of the **Embered Hand** has grown powerful in the Beacon's absence, claiming that the volcano's heat is the true light and the Beacon was a false god. Their hold on the community is genuine but fragile.

### The Beacon Shard

Inside the volcanic caldera, in a pocket of cooled rock at the center — protected by extreme heat rather than shadow. Getting there requires navigating the volcano's interior and dealing with the Embered Hand cultists, who aren't villains so much as frightened people who built a theology around survival.

### The Umbral Guardian — The Smothered Flame

A creature that looks like fire that has been extinguished — embers suspended in a dark cloud, reaching, trying to burn but unable to. It's the most visually striking Guardian: the shapes within it look almost like Lumins trying to escape.

### After the Beacon

The Beacon here burns deep red-amber, warm and volcanic. The cult dissolves not in conflict but in wonder — seeing living light beside volcanic heat makes the difference obvious and undeniable. Cinder, the young acolyte, becomes the region's new informal Lightkeeper.

## Region 5 — The Verdant Tangle

**Biome**: Ancient rainforest, massive canopy trees, elevated walkway city built in the branches
**Culture**: Arborists, naturalists, a community that lives above the forest floor entirely
**Beacon State**: Dark for 5 years — the longest among the first five regions

### Before the Beacon

The Tangle in darkness is eerie. The forest below the canopy city is pitch black — not even bioluminescence, which has faded. The canopy city is lit by firelight and maintained with anxious determination. Grandma Moss, the 90-year-old matriarch, is the only living person who remembers the Beacon burning and has spent five years insisting it can be restored, to diminishing audiences.

### The Beacon Shard

At the base of the oldest tree in the forest — the **Heartwood**, hundreds of meters tall, with roots the size of buildings. The forest floor dungeon is the game's largest and most complex: completely dark except for Lumin light, with environmental puzzles that require using multiple Lumins with different light properties.

### The Umbral Guardian — The Canopy Void

Unlike the other Guardians, the Canopy Void has no fixed form. It is an absence — a moving patch of absolute darkness in the forest floor that absorbs all light directed at it. The fight involves surrounding it with Lumin light rather than attacking it directly.

### After the Beacon

The Tangle erupts with green and gold. The canopy filters the Beacon light into thousands of shifting patterns on the forest floor. Bioluminescent plants re-emerge overnight. The forest floor, sealed off for five years, becomes safe and beautiful. Grandma Moss descends from the canopy city for the first time in years to walk on the ground.

## Region 6 — Frostmere

**Biome**: Glacial tundra, ice caverns, aurora skies, underground settlements
**Culture**: Deeply self-sufficient, introverted, have largely abandoned surface life — subterranean cities lit by mushrooms
**Beacon State**: Dark for decades — no living adult remembers it burning

### Before the Beacon

Frostmere's surface is breathtaking in a cold, hostile way — vast white and grey. The people live underground. They are not unhappy, exactly, but they carry a heaviness that they don't have a name for. Children born here have never seen the stars clearly; the perpetual overcast that replaced the Beacon's warmth means they rarely see the sky at all.

### The Beacon Shard

Encased in the deepest glacier — frozen inside a wall of ice that has been growing for decades. Recovering it requires the most elaborate puzzle of the game: redirecting Lumin heat to melt a specific path through ancient ice.

### The Umbral Guardian — The Long Night

The oldest, most powerful Guardian — formed from decades of accumulated darkness. It is barely recognizable as a creature; it is almost just an area of the world that is wrong. The fight is the game's most difficult and is the only one where the after-defeat moment feels like the Guardian is *relieved* rather than dissolved.

### After the Beacon

Frostmere's Beacon burns cool silver-white, like starlight concentrated. The sky clears for the first time in living memory. Children run to the surface to see it. The most affecting moment in the game: Sable, who has been traveling with Luma for the entire journey, sees their home lit up for the first time and breaks down completely. Keeper Brin, Sable's old teacher, stands next to them and says nothing — just puts a hand on their shoulder.

## Region 7 — The Beacon Heartland

**Biome**: High plateau, ancient Lightweaver ruins, ethereal, between-states
**Culture**: No permanent population — visited by Lightkeepers on pilgrimage, now abandoned
**Beacon State**: Sealed — not dark, not bright, a grey stasis

### Before the Seal is Broken

The Heartland is the strangest place in Solara. Light behaves oddly here — it doesn't cast proper shadows. The ruins are enormous and beautiful, clearly built for ceremony rather than habitation. The Prime Beacon Tower is taller than any other, but its flame chamber is sealed with a substance that is neither stone nor shadow — a grey membrane of crystallized Lightweaver essence.

### The Final Encounter

No traditional dungeon. The Heartland is a journey through the Lightweavers' history — murals, records, and echo-memories (environmental storytelling) that reveal Solen's story. The final encounter is inside the sealed Beacon itself: a space that exists between light and dark, where Solen waits.

### After the Prime Beacon

The entire world lights up simultaneously. Every Beacon Tower in Solara flares at once. The membrane dissolves. Solen is free. The game's final sequence shows brief vignettes of each region — Willowfen, the Ashfields, Tidehollow, Emberveil, the Tangle, Frostmere — all blazing with light, all the people we met looking up.

The Prime Beacon's light is all colours at once.
