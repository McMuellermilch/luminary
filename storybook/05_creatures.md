# Lumins — Creatures of Solara

## What Are Lumins?

Lumins are creatures intrinsically connected to living light. They evolved alongside the Beacon Towers over millennia, drawn to the warmth of Lightweaver essence. Every Lumin carries a small internal light source — not fire, not electricity, but the same living light that burns in the Beacons. This is why they cannot survive indefinitely in complete darkness: they slowly dim, grow anxious, and eventually lose coherence.

Lumins are not magical animals in the fantasy sense — they are simply creatures whose biology is built around light. They photosynthesize ambient light through special cells called **lumicytes**. In dark regions, lumicytes starve. This is why Lumins in unlit regions become hostile or reclusive: they are suffering.

When a Beacon is relit, Lumins recover almost immediately. Their lumicytes flood with energy. This is also why Lumins bond with Luma — her inner light is a steady, warm food source for lumicytes. A Lumin bonded to Luma never goes hungry, even in the darkest region.

---

## Lumin Biology

**Lumicytes**: Light-storing cells present in every Lumin. Most visible as glowing patches, rings, or auras on the creature's body. In healthy Lumins, these glow brightly and may shift colour with emotion. In starved Lumins, they appear dim or grey.

**The Bond**: When a Lumin establishes a bond with a person, their lumicytes sync to that person's emotional state. Bonded Lumins glow brighter when their partner is happy, dim when sad, flicker when frightened. This makes Lumins extraordinarily expressive companions.

**Evolution**: Lumins evolve through extended bonding and accumulated light exposure. Evolution is not triggered by combat — it occurs when a Lumin has spent enough time in positive emotional resonance with their partner and has absorbed sufficient light. In dark regions, evolution slows or pauses.

## Lumin Types (Elemental Affinities)

Lumins are loosely organized into elemental families based on which environmental light source their ancestors were adapted to.

| Type | Source | Characteristic | Behaviour in Dark |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Flare** | Sunlight / volcanic light | Warm, intense, bold | Aggressive and hot-tempered |
| **Tide** | Moonlight / sea reflection | Gentle, flowing, patient | Reclusive, retreats underwater |
| **Verdant** | Forest-filtered light | Nurturing, slow, wise | Hibernates in deep roots |
| **Frost** | Starlight / ice reflection | Calm, precise, distant | Slowest to dim, last to break |
| **Spark** | Lightning / storm energy | Erratic, quick, joyful | Panicked, flies randomly |
| **Dusk** | Twilight / amber glow | Balanced, adaptable | Most resilient in darkness |
| **Void** | *(corrupted)* | Rare, forms only in absolute darkness | Born hostile — can be redeemed post-Beacon |

Note: **Void Lumins** are not evil. They are Lumins born in regions with no Beacon, whose lumicytes developed around ambient shadow rather than light. Post-rekindling, Void Lumins can reform — their shadow energy shifting toward genuine light. They are the rarest and most complex companions.

## Creature Roster (Initial 24 Lumins for MVP + Full Game)

### Flare Family

**Pip / Flamewing / Solwing**
Luma's starting companion. Ember-moth with stained-glass wings.
- Pip: Small, excitable, warm orange glow. Fast, low HP.
- Flamewing: Larger, wings now full solar panels. Gains Radiance ability.
- Solwing: Majestic, wingspan wide as a person's arms. Living sunlight made form. Rare evolution requiring Beacon Heartland light.

**Cinderpup / Blazehound**
A small dog-like creature made of warm embers and dark fur.
- Dark regions: barks constantly, cannot calm down.
- Lit regions: playful, will fetch things.

**Ashwalker**
Tall, flamingo-like bird with feathers that look like cooling lava. Solitary. Found only in Emberveil.

### Tide Family

**Gleamfin / Shimmray**
Fish-like Lumin with bioluminescent patterns that pulse with their mood.
- Found in rivers and coastal areas.
- Dark regions: burrows into river mud, emits distress pulses.

**Lumenal**
A gentle, whale-shaped Lumin that floats through the air like a slow balloon. Enormous and docile. Found near Tidehollow cliffs.

**Pearlite**
A tiny crab-like creature whose shell is a perfect reflective sphere. Bounces light around in complex patterns. Tidehollow exclusive.

### Verdant Family

**Mossling / Rootwise / Grovekeeper**
Turtle-like creature with a shell covered in living moss and tiny plants.
- Mossling: Baby, shy, hides in shell frequently.
- Rootwise: Adolescent, shell plants have grown into small flowers.
- Grovekeeper: Elder form. The shell has become a small ecosystem. Very slow. Enormous HP.

**Fernwhistle**
A tall, slender deer-like creature with antlers made of living wood. Eyes are amber lanterns. Only appears in deep forest.

**Spriglet / Bloomweave**
A small humanoid figure made of woven grass and flower petals. Common in Verdant Tangle. Bond with children in lit regions.

### Frost Family

**Crystick / Glacewing**
Insect-like creature with wings made of thin ice that refract light into rainbows.
- Dark regions: wings frost over entirely, cannot fly.
- Lit regions: constantly creates small rainbows nearby.

**Snowdrift**
A fox-like creature that appears to be made of drifting snow. Leaves no footprints. Extremely fast.

**Aurorel**
Jellyfish-like creature that floats at night and whose tendrils create aurora patterns. Only appears at night in Frostmere after the Beacon is relit.

### Spark Family

**Zippet / Stormwing**
Tiny bird made of electricity. Moves faster than the eye can track.
- Dark regions: discharges randomly and uncontrollably, zapping everything nearby.
- Lit regions: playful, leaves tiny light trails when it flies.

**Bolthorn**
A rhino-like creature covered in lightning-rod horns. Extremely tough. Rare.

### Dusk Family

**Glimmer / Lumara**
The most common bonded Lumin type. Small, roundish, with large eyes and a soft glow that changes colour with emotion.
- Universally beloved. Found in every region.
- Glimmer: Baby form. Very soft light.
- Lumara: Adult form. Light strong enough to read by. Makes an excellent night companion.

**Twycandl**
A candle-like creature — small, waxy body, tiny flame on its head. The flame cannot be extinguished in normal conditions. Socially bonds with humans more readily than most Lumins.

### Void Family (Post-Beacon, Rare)

**Umbrit / Veilwarden**
A reformed Void Lumin. Before rekindling: a small, hostile shadow-shape. After rekindling: a beautiful creature of deep purple and silver, like a small piece of the night sky.
- Umbrit: Skittish, won't approach. Must be captured with patience rather than combat.
- Veilwarden: Serene, luminous. One of the strongest Lumins in the game.

## Behavior in Dark vs Lit Regions

| State | Appearance | In Battle | Out of Battle |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Dark region, unbonded** | Dim, grey-tinged, erratic glow | Aggressive or panicked | Runs from player or attacks |
| **Dark region, bonded** | Full glow from Luma's light | Normal, steady | Calm near Luma, cautious of surroundings |
| **Lit region, unbonded** | Full colour, healthy glow | Skittish but non-aggressive | Can be approached for capture |
| **Lit region, bonded** | Brightest possible glow | Peak performance | Playful, expressive, relaxed |

**Post-Beacon changes**: After a Beacon is relit, previously-hostile Lumins in that region calm immediately. Some that fled return within hours. Rare species emerge that were in hiding. The encounter tables for a region change significantly after its Beacon is relit.

## Capture Mechanics (Lumin-Specific)

Lumins cannot be captured in standard ways — they won't enter any containment by force. Instead, Luma uses **Lightglass Lanterns**: small glass vessels that contain a fragment of Beacon light. A Lumin offered a Lantern (thrown near them when their health is low) will voluntarily enter if they trust the light inside.

This means:
- Capture is always voluntary from the Lumin's perspective
- Lumins with very dim lumicytes (low HP) are most receptive — they need the light
- Void Lumins require special Lanterns called **Duskglass** that contain shadow-light
- In dark regions, capture is harder (Lumins are more suspicious)
- In lit regions, capture is easier (Lumins are curious and well-fed)

The capture UI shows a trust meter rather than a probability percentage — it feels like coaxing rather than gambling.
